Project snapshot

Problem

Todays 5G handover security is based on public‑key algorithms that large quantum computers could break. An attacker who records encrypted traffic now could decrypt it years later once such machines exist.

click to expand

Goal

Build a handover procedure that keeps latency and signaling overhead inside 5G limits while providing strong protection against both classical and quantum attacks.

click to expand

Scope

  • RAN and 5G core handover signaling only.
  • Post‑quantum ML‑KEM for key establishment, ML‑DSA for signing.
  • Compare against baseline 5G handover in latency and message size.

Step 1 · Capability & policy

The network advertises that it supports post‑quantum safe handover. Policy decides when to enable it (for example for enterprise slices or high‑value subscribers).
